## What Are the Most Effective Ways to Block Minecraft Bedrock Hack Clients?

The best strategy is a layered one that combines server security, automatic tools, and active staff oversight.

**On the Network Level:**
- Set up firewalls to block suspicious connection attempts.
- Use rate limiting to stop coordinated login spam.
- Hide your server's real IP address with a proxy or VPN service.

**With Automated Systems:**
- Install anti-cheat plugins that analyze how players are acting.
- Use dedicated tools like the 'mcbe-anti-cheat' project on GitHub, which works for Minecraft Bedrock 1.19 and up.
- Try advanced add-ons like Matrix AntiCheat to catch specific cheats like fly, no-clip, speed, and kill aura.

## How Do Anti-Cheat Systems Find Minecraft Bedrock Hacks?

Anti-cheat plugins watch what players do in real-time, looking for actions that simply aren't possible in the normal game. This is a clear sign someone is using a modified client.

**Behavioral Red Flags:**
- **Movement:** Is a player moving impossibly fast or teleporting?
- **Jumping:** Are they jumping higher than the game allows?
- **Mining:** Do they mine in perfectly straight lines directly to valuable resources, suggesting X-ray?
- **Combat:** Are they landing every hit with perfect aim (kill aura) or hitting through walls?

**Technical Fingerprints:**
- Looking for the digital "signatures" of known cheat programs.
- Spotting modified game files or other unauthorized changes.
- Catching automated inputs that are too fast or precise for a human player.

For example, the 'smell-of-curry/mcbe-anti-cheat' project is built to catch common Minecraft Bedrock cheats like fly hacks, jetpacks, speed hacks, nukers, and command block exploits.

## What Manual Signs of Cheating Should I Look For?

Automated systems are great, but they can't catch everything. Keeping an eye out for suspicious activity yourself is key to nabbing clever cheaters.

**In-Game Behavior to Watch:**
- **Mining:** Look for players who mine in unnaturally straight lines toward diamonds or other valuables. Are they way more efficient than anyone else? Do they stumble upon rare ores with zero exploration?
- **Movement:** Watch for players zipping around too fast, moving through walls (no-clip), or jumping and flying in ways the game doesn't allow.
- **Combat:** Be suspicious of players with flawless aim, impossible reaction speeds, or the ability to hit others through solid blocks. Repetitive, machine-like clicking during fights is another dead giveaway.

## How Can I Make My Server More Secure Overall?

To truly protect your server from Minecraft Bedrock hack clients, you need to combine technical tools with smart server management.

**On the Technical Side:**
- Keep your server software updated to patch the latest security holes.
- Use systems that flag strange player behavior automatically.
- Have a good backup and recovery plan in case a cheater causes damage.

**On the People Side:**
- Have active moderators online, especially during busy times, to respond quickly.
- Set clear rules about cheating and enforce the consequences.
- Create an easy way for your community to report players they think are cheating.

**For Long-Term Health:**
- Regularly check your anti-cheat logs and fairly review any ban appeals.
- Keep your anti-cheat rules updated as new cheats are developed.
- Watch your server's performance to make sure your security tools aren't causing lag.
